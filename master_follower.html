01 Shipped Work · Example 1

Keeping linked requirements in sync, across every role

Between meetings, documents and emails, decision makers and engineers can lose track of decisions that are critical. A change in a Master requirement propagates automatically to its Follower requirements.

One change on the Master. Every Follower flagged. Nothing updates silently.

The shipped feature: master and follower connections per requirement. Source: Altium 365 documentation

01 · Context

Four roles depend on the same requirement. A decision by one is critical to the others.

Engineering consequences are costly, constraints are physical and legal, so decisions and reviews need to be accountable and transparent. Master-Follower defines high impact requirements (Master) and their dependent requirements (Follower).

02 · My role

Before a PM was hired, the translation of the spoken word into writing and design was solely my job.

Collaboration was direct with users, Subject Matter Experts, CPO and CTO. I anchored an unknown domain through interviews: what is the biggest frustration, what is the day to day reality, can you show me? I wrote user stories, then translated them into design requirements: smaller pieces, individual UI behaviours.

03 · Key design decision

A Follower never updates silently.

The key decision was clarity at the moment of propagation: what does the engineer receiving a change want to know or decide right then? Propagation is unidirectional, the change is flagged on the Follower, and its owner applies or rejects it.

04 · Alignment across roles

Complex enough to be useful in highly specialized work, simple enough to be feasible.

Weekly meetings, feedback sessions and presentations kept CPO, CTO and Subject Matter Experts aligned. I was responsible for getting the information I needed, designing, iterating and updating everyone.

05 · Lessons learned

My modesty led me to design a feature that was too complex.

Only a few months into my first product design job, I thought it must be me who could hardly handle the complexity of systems engineering logic. It was feasible, but less feasible than anticipated. On the next feature I asked for user story collaboration and agreement on the most crucial requirements from the beginning: see Use Case: Verification Assignment Matrix.

06 · Outcome

Shipped at Valispace in 2021. Migrated and still live in Altium 365 in 2026.
