03 · 0→1 · New domain and user discovery

I came into systems engineering as a non expert, and used that.

The translation of the spoken word into user stories and design was solely my job. My non expert status let me ask the questions a fellow engineer might assume instead of validate, or be embarrassed to ask. Experts often can't explain their assumptions without the right prompts, so I built the feature logic from expert and user interviews instead of documentation.

Fast prototyping, frequent testing · startup pace

04 · Constraints

  • New domain for me and no PM yet · direct collaboration with clients, users, SMEs, CPO and CTO
  • No UX practice in the company · I introduced Figma, interviews and prototype testing while designing
  • Expert users spread across companies and time zones, partly in Covid lockdown

05 · Approach

UX methodology to put user needs at the centre, from scratch.

Interviews per role, user journeys, wireframes, then a hi fi Figma prototype tested with expert users and iterated. Alongside the designs I drafted the knowledge in notes and user stories, so decisions were traceable to what users said, not to opinions in a meeting.

06 · Key decisions

  • Propagate as flags, not silent updates · the owner of each Follower applies or rejects the change
  • Accessible status indicators readable by every role, not only engineers
  • Hi fi prototype as the alignment object between internal experts, external users and decision makers

07 · Impact

  • Contract signed · one of three systems in systems features that won a six figure contract
  • Shipped 2021 · kept after the acquisition · live in Altium 365 in 2026
  • The approach was integrated in the company, and I was promoted to the newly founded Product team
  • I later advocated for, drafted the job description and interviewed the next designer, still at Altium 3.5 years later

08 · Lessons learned

Requirements and designs should not come from the same person. In the next feature I asked the CPO to take over the requirements while I translated them into design. See Use Case 2, Verification Assignment Matrix.

09 · Relevant post AI in UX design

This was designed pre AI. Today AI would speed up first drafts, but new domain discovery still needs a human asking the non expert questions: AI knows job roles generally, not how a specific company operates, and experts still need the right prompts to surface what they assume.
