04 · Process · from workflow to MVP

The hard part wasn't the matrix — it was getting everyone to agree on what was Must-have.

Applying the lesson from Use Case 1, I reduced the feature to an MVP by insisting we decide, write and review the Must-have requirements first — and gradually pushed for others to write the requirements too, rather than carrying them alone.

For the first time, design and requirements were agreed with the client, not only the design and a high-level idea. It took patience and insistence to bring the user perspective to agreement on what was truly Must-have — while keeping in mind what would still be crucial later, which is what prevented development refactoring.

05 · Key design decisions

The central question I kept asking: which job role needs which view and filter? An engineer working on one verification method filters per method; a decision maker reviewing a requirement set filters for its status. Same matrix, different scenarios.

Views and filters per roleengineers filter one method, decision makers filter a set's status
Connected to the requirementsthe link in the software is what beats a separate Excel matrix
MVP boundarya small system that works and extends, not a big one that needs rework
Assignment loggedwho and when, replacing status meetings and email chains
Manual and automatic methodsrules and test update with the data; the rest are set by hand

06 · Lessons learned

A hi-fi prototype is a discovery tool, not just a deliverable.

Its detail level surfaces the questions that define the MVP — what if one method isn't done yet, do you need to know who was assigned and reach them? Designing the small system that works beats specifying the complete one that doesn't ship.

I used my non-expert status to ask instead of assume. An expert-as-designer risks leaving their own bias undetected, or hesitating to question a senior engineer or client.

AI knows job roles generally, not a company's specifics — so AI-generated prototypes still need testing with real users, and continuous cycles without human oversight grow scope creep and inconsistencies fastest.
