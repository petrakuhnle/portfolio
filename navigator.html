⬡ Navigator · Sound AI Regulations App

AI compliance tools for musicians, labels, and sound designers.

2025 to 2026 · EU AI Act · GDPR · European Accessibility Act

Problem

Musicians and labels have to comply with the EU AI Act and GDPR. The rules are dense and written for lawyers, not creators.

Solution

A React decision tree that turns a use case into clear requirements, risks, and steps. Behind it sits an open source, source checked guide.

Impact

V1 is live on GitHub Pages. It passed round 1 of the Mozilla Fellowship 2026. Two backend engineers joined to build V2.

AI and human: who does what

I tracked the split as I built. Here is what the AI handled and what I owned.

AI assisted

Speed and first drafts

  • First summaries of long regulatory text
  • React scaffolding and component stubs
  • Rewording dense clauses in plainer language
  • Suggesting edge cases to check
Human led

Judgment and verification

  • Reading the primary sources and confirming every claim
  • Deciding which rules apply to which cases
  • The decision tree logic and its branches
  • Product scope, tone, and what to leave out

Reflection

One limit I have not solved: regulations change. V2 aims to auto update from official sources. That is engineering work, so for now the tool ages over time.
