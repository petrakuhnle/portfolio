08

Addressed

  • Saves non-legal people the time and confusion of searching the open web. The decision tree gives a first orientation on what might apply — so you are not left wondering whether protection is needed for cases that do not need it.

  • Puts UX and accessibility first, so getting to the information feels lighter from the start — the aim is to ease friction, not stack more legal and technical hurdles on top of an already dense topic.

  • Does not make the decision for you. It lays out the relevant information transparently and accurately, and leaves the call in your hands.

  • Covers the main use cases, so anyone — facing a specific situation or just curious — can build a sense of how the law, the technology, and AI fit together.

09

Opportunities

  • Use an LLM to translate legal language into something plainer while keeping it transparent — for example, always linking back to the original source.

  • Build in auto-updating, since AI law changes often and the guidance needs to keep pace with frequent updates.

  • Keep that updating honest about its limits — clear on the line between information and legal advice, and between a plain-language representation and something a legal professional has actually validated.
