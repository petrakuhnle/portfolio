03 · How it works

One use case in. The obligations and steps that apply come out.

The rules are dense and written for lawyers, not creators. The decision tree asks about AI involvement, personal data and distribution, matches the answers to the rules and returns tailored requirements and action steps.

04 · Approach

Research, structure, verify, build, publish.

  • Research: primary regulations, not summaries
  • Structure: rules mapped to real use cases
  • Verify: sources checked before anything ships
  • Build: prototype with design tools and code · publish on GitHub Pages

05 · Relevance / AI

AI sped things up. The thinking and direction were mine.

  • AI assisted: first summaries of long regulatory text, React scaffolding, rewording dense clauses, suggesting edge cases
  • Human led: topic and scope, the design and the decision tree logic (which rules map to which cases), UX and accessibility choices, prompting and deciding what to consider
  • The tool does not decide for you: it lays out the relevant information transparently and leaves the call in your hands

06 · Impact

  • V1 live on GitHub Pages
  • Mozilla Fellowship 2026 nominee · passed round 1
  • 2 engineers joined for V2

07 · Opportunities

  • Use an LLM to translate legal language into plainer terms while always linking back to the original source
  • Auto updating, since AI law changes often and the guidance needs to keep pace
  • Stay honest about limits: information is not legal advice, and plain language is not legally validated wording
