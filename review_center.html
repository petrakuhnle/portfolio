01 Shipped Work · Example 3

Review decisions, traceable inside a regulated workflow

A formal review is common practice in engineering work. The Review Center gives an overview of review assignment and completion, with more assignment, view and filter options, interconnected with the rest of the engineering project.

Feature: Review Center Valispace → Altium 365 Designed 2021 · Live 2026

The moderator sets up the review, reviewers rate each object, the approver closes it with a timestamp. Every rating is logged.

The shipped product: requirement state and V&V status at a glance

01 · Context

Assigning reviews to specific employees or groups can be time consuming.

Who needs to review what by what time? Are the reviews done, or lagging behind the timeline of the project? A project can involve 1000s of requirements and 100s of people, similar people are assigned to similar requirements over and over, reviews may need to be redone, and the communication about them is often tracked in another software, in meetings or per email. The integrated Review Center gives a better overview and faster assignments, directly on the data that already exists in the project.

02 · From requirements to design

Breaking down vague verbal descriptions into concrete user stories and UI elements.

Requirements were written from expert and expert user input. My task was to co-write, edit and further specify high level feature requirements into specific design requirements and designs: exactly what the person who assigns needs to be able to select, down to wording, UI behaviour, icons and colors. The concrete, detailed level of a Figma prototype brings up questions that feed back into user and expert interviews, so we could iterate and refine until enough use cases were covered for the feature to be useful.

03 · Key design decision

Assign groups of reviewers, not only individuals.

Reviews repeat. The feature aims at reducing repetition, to reduce the time a task costs over a month or over a project, not only the time to complete one specific task in the software.

04 · Working with development

The questions had to be asked from the logic of the user, not yet the logic of the code.

Experts and I mapped out the logic; development took over the user stories and designs as a starting point. A backend developer later fed back that the logic was solid, since new feedback was easy to integrate into it. The work was highly logical and systems based rather than visual polish. A complex design with the user logic as reference, combined with development's flexibility to find the best backend and frontend solution, worked well. Interviewing and testing several expert users was what allowed us to generalize enough to know what to build.

05 · Lessons learned

Involve experts and expert users early, and design earlier still.

Design being involved from the first interviews, drafts of user stories and first sketches was the best approach to a realistic and user friendly version. What we could have done better: a middle step of first code prototypes, feeding development constraints back into the existing designs instead of going straight to code. For expert software, all roles need to acknowledge their dependency on true collaboration, which we proved by the features still living in Altium 365 today.

06 · Outcome

Shipped at Valispace in 2021. Migrated and still live in Altium 365 in 2026.
