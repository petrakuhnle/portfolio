How I found the MVP of a complex feature with user stories and hi fi prototypes

My work: translating expert requirements into user stories, flows and a hi fi prototype that defined the MVP. The feature: one matrix to assign, track and communicate verification methods per requirement.

03 · From workflow to user flow

Users already worked in a matrix, just outside the product.

Decisions lived in Excel, docs, meetings and emails, disconnected from the requirements they were about. I asked each role how they work now, which parts are worth integrating and which choices had to be added. Applying the lesson from Use Case 1, the CPO owned the requirements and I owned the translation into design, so expert accuracy and design came from two people.

Digital to integrated digital · the workflow becomes a user flow

04 · Constraints

  • Regulated work environment · decisions must be traceable and reliable
  • Many roles with opposite needs: overview for managers, focus for specialists
  • Complex enough to be useful, small enough to build · cognitive load against information need

05 · Approach

User stories first, then journey, wireframes, interactive prototype.

I pushed for decisions based on user stories, not only designs. Requirements and the hi fi prototype both separated the MVP from the future version, which turned out crucial for planning the backend. The concrete detail level of the prototype raised questions that fed back into expert interviews, making every design decision transparent and concrete.

06 · Key decisions

  • MVP boundary: design a small system that works and is extendable, not a big one that needs rework
  • Filters per method, per requirement set and per status · one matrix serving opposite roles
  • Assignments logged with who and when, replacing status meetings and email chains

07 · Impact

  • Designed and built in significantly less time than the first feature
  • Users told Solution Management we integrated their real workflow in a useful way
  • Shipped 2021 · live in Altium 365 in 2026

08 · Lessons learned

A hi fi prototype is a discovery tool, not just a deliverable: its detail level surfaces the questions that define the MVP. Designing the small system that works beats designing the complete system that doesn't ship.

09 · Relevant post AI in UX design

I haven't yet fed 100s of requirements to AI to generate a prototype, and in this field data security would constrain it. What I know from continuous iteration cycles: without human oversight and alignment between humans, scope creep and inconsistencies grow, and an AI generated prototype still needs testing with individual users.
