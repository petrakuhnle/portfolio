01 · What changed after Master-Follower

This time, requirements and designs did not come from the same person.

I asked the CPO to take over the written requirements, while I took on the translation into design requirements, prototypes, feedback sessions and iterations. This ensured expert accuracy. I advocated for decisions based on user stories as well as designs, and drilled down to an MVP while understanding the future version of the feature.

02 · Key design decision

Which job roles need which views and filters.

An engineer working on one kind of verification filters per method. A decision maker reviewing a requirement set filters for the status of its verifications. The connectedness to the requirements in the software is what makes the matrix more useful than an Excel matrix.

04 · Reflection

I used my non expert status to ask questions instead of making or skipping assumptions.

A fellow engineer might assume instead of validate, or hesitate to ask a more experienced engineer or high level client. The risk of an expert as designer is that the expert bias remains undetected or needs to be consciously validated.

AI can know job roles generally, but not the company specific details of how people operate. AI generated prototypes still need testing with individual users, and continuous feedback cycles without human oversight cause scope creep and inconsistencies most easily. Design here is about making the abstract concrete, imagining roles and scenarios and asking about them: what if one verification method is not done yet, do you need to know who was assigned and communicate with them?
