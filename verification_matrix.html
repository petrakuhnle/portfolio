Feature: Verification Assignment Matrix

This feature allows managers and engineers to assign and communicate about different verification methods per requirement or requirement set.

In real life, several verification methods are assigned to be done per requirement or requirement set. These can be agreed between companies or within a company. But the verification isn't done by the person and needs to be verifiable and reliable. This Matrix allows users to get a better overview, assign and communicate about the assigned methods in compact matrix view instead of tables, meetings, docs and emails.

01 · Context

Managing, assigning and verifying the verification that needs to be done per requirement may be done by one person and in a slightly less fromal process in the beginning or in smaller companies, but whent the company size grows or the project grows keeping track of verifications to be done and being done gets tricky very quickly. With MBSE engineers have moved to a more interconnected and interdependent digital working model, yet many decisions may still be double checked or managed on sheets, docs, in meetings or emails. This feature allows to keep track of those decisions and their execution for everyone who is impacted by it.

02 · From requirements to design

Learning from the Master-Follower Design, I advocated for decisions not only based on designs but user stories as well. And very importantly, I was more skilled and confident in drilling down to an MVP version while also understanding the future version of the feature to avoid scope creep. This way we had written requirements for the feature and a designed Hi-Fi Prototype and the requirements and the prototype did already consider the MVP vs future version. This turned out to be crucial for the planning of the Backend structure and one crucial detail was caught by me as designer since the designs requirement me to pay very close attention to every detail of the user and expert feedback about the needs of the engineers for this specific feature. Most important was what type of job roles needed to see different types of views and filters. The challenges were to show a extensive enough view to create an overview, while also allowing to focus and filter to only the requirements or verification necessary.

04 · Key design decision

The key decisions for this feature were around what and how many filter and view options are needed for the feature to be useful enough for engineers. The feature aimed at being more useful than an Excel Matrix because of it it's connectedness to the requirements and verification decisions that were directly linked to the project itself in the software.

05 · Alignment across roles

Crucial was to align on what type of main responsibilities and main job roles needed to be considered for this feature. Imagine one engineer is mainly working on a specific kind of verification, therefore the feature needs to allow the user to filter per verification method. A decision maker needs to review, if all verification methods for a specific requirement set have passed, therefore the feature needs to allow the user to filter for the status of the verfications per requirement set.

06 · Outcome

Shipped at Valispace 2021; migrated and still live in Altium 365 (2026). Feature and requirements were agreed on in writing, funding secured and built by development in 2021.

07 Lessons learned

In this feature, I was the one pusing to apply the lessons learned in -> Use Case Master-Follower and this worked. The feature was designed and built in significantly less time and the users also provided positive feedback to our Solution Management team about the feature, explaining that we integrated a part of their workflow in a useful way into our product. The idea began to form, when I asked questions to users about 'How are they working now?' and understanding what parts are useful to integrate & what choices need to be added based on user experience discovery per job role in relations to decision making and completion about verification methods for requirements.
